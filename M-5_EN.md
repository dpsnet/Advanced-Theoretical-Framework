## 2 Algebraic Foundation of Time

### 2.1 Special Status of Time Dimension in Clifford Algebra

Based on the Clifford algebra theory of [M-1], we consider the Clifford algebra $\mathcal{Cl}(3,1)$ for 4D spacetime, with signature $(3,1)$, i.e., 3 spatial dimensions with positive sign and 1 time dimension with negative sign. This signature difference directly leads to fundamentally different algebraic properties between time and spatial dimensions.

**Definition 2.1.1** (Clifford Algebra Generators): Based on the Clifford algebra theory of [M-1], the Clifford algebra $\mathcal{Cl}(3,1)$ for 4D spacetime is generated by generators $\gamma^0, \gamma^1, \gamma^2, \gamma^3$, satisfying the following fundamental anticommutation relations:
$$\{\gamma^\mu, \gamma^\nu\} = 2g^{\mu\nu}I$$
where $g^{\mu\nu}$ is the Minkowski metric, $g^{00}=-1$, $g^{ii}=1$ ($i=1,2,3$), $g^{\mu\nu}=0$ ($\mu\neq\nu$). Here $\{A,B\}=AB+BA$ denotes the anticommutator, and $I$ is the identity element.

**Theorem 2.1.1** (Square of Time Generator): The square of the time generator $\gamma^0$ is the negative identity matrix, while the square of spatial generators $\gamma^i$ is the positive identity matrix:
$$(\gamma^0)^2 = -I, \quad (\gamma^i)^2 = I \quad (i=1,2,3)$$

**Proof**: According to the fundamental anticommutation relations of Clifford algebra $\{\gamma^\mu, \gamma^\nu\} = 2g^{\mu\nu}I$,

1. For the time generator $\gamma^0$, let $\mu=\nu=0$, then:
   $$\{\gamma^0, \gamma^0\} = 2g^{00}I = 2(-1)I = -2I$$
   Since the anticommutator is defined as $\{\gamma^0, \gamma^0\} = \gamma^0\gamma^0 + \gamma^0\gamma^0 = 2(\gamma^0)^2$, therefore:
   $$2(\gamma^0)^2 = -2I \implies (\gamma^0)^2 = -I$$

2. For spatial generators $\gamma^i$, let $\mu=\nu=i$, then:
   $$\{\gamma^i, \gamma^i\} = 2g^{ii}I = 2(1)I = 2I$$
   Similarly, the anticommutator $\{\gamma^i, \gamma^i\} = 2(\gamma^i)^2$, therefore:
   $$2(\gamma^i)^2 = 2I \implies (\gamma^i)^2 = I$$

**Corollary 2.1.1**: The time generator $\gamma^0$ is anti-Hermitian, while spatial generators $\gamma^i$ are Hermitian:
$$(\gamma^0)^\dagger = -\gamma^0, \quad (\gamma^i)^\dagger = \gamma^i \quad (i=1,2,3)$$

**Proof**: Based on the Clifford algebra representation theory of [M-1], the Hermiticity of generators is directly related to the metric signature:

1. **Hermiticity Definition**: An operator $A$ is Hermitian if and only if $A^\dagger = A$; it is anti-Hermitian if and only if $A^\dagger = -A$.

2. **Trace Conditions for Generators**: For representations of Clifford algebra, generators satisfy $\text{Tr}(\gamma^\mu) = 0$ (zero trace), and $\text{Tr}(\gamma^\mu\gamma^\nu) = 4g^{\mu\nu}$ ([M-1]).

3. **Time Generator $\gamma^0$**: Since $(\gamma^0)^2 = -I$, its eigenvalues must be $\pm i$ (purely imaginary). According to the property in quantum mechanics that Hermitian operators must have real eigenvalues, $\gamma^0$ cannot be Hermitian. Further calculation of its Hermitian conjugate:
   $$(\gamma^0)^\dagger = -\gamma^0$$
   Verification: $(\gamma^0)^\dagger (\gamma^0) = (-\gamma^0)\gamma^0 = -(-I) = I$, satisfying the unitarity condition.

4. **Spatial Generators $\gamma^i$**: Since $(\gamma^i)^2 = I$, their eigenvalues are $\pm 1$ (real). According to the definition of Hermitian operators in quantum mechanics, $\gamma^i$ is Hermitian:
   $$(\gamma^i)^\dagger = \gamma^i$$
   Verification: $(\gamma^i)^\dagger (\gamma^i) = \gamma^i\gamma^i = I$, satisfying the unitarity condition.
