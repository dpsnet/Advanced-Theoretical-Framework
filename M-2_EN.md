1. **Fixed Topological Dimension**: The topological dimension of spacetime (e.g., 4 dimensions) is a fixed intrinsic property that does not change with energy scale, measure, or observation scale, ensuring fundamental compatibility with classical general relativity.

2. **Dynamic Spectral Dimension**: The spectral dimension is a dynamical variable describing the "effective diffusion dimension" of spacetime geometry at different energy scales. It **only reflects the distribution characteristics of the measure field**, not the topological dimension of spacetime itself. The definition of spectral dimension is strictly based on heat kernel diffusion:
   $$d_s = -2\lim_{t\to0^+}\frac{\ln\text{Tr}(e^{-tD^2})}{\ln t}$$
   where $D$ is the Dirac operator and $t$ is diffusion time.

3. **Elimination of Measure-Dimension Confusion**: The fractal properties of measures (Ahlfors regularity or multifractality) lead to changes in spectral dimension at different energy scales. This change is a pure **measure effect**, unrelated to the topological dimension of spacetime. The so-called "non-integer dimension" characteristics are actually manifestations of energy-scale-dependent spectral dimensions under fractal measures, **not changes in the topological dimension of spacetime**.

#### 1.2.1 Construction and Properties of Hausdorff Measure

Let $(X,d)$ be a metric space and $s \geq 0$. For $\delta > 0$, define the $\delta$-covering Hausdorff measure:
$$\mathcal{H}^s_\delta(E) = \inf\left\{\sum_i (\text{diam } U_i)^s : E \subset \bigcup_i U_i, \text{diam } U_i < \delta\right\}$$

**Definition**: The $s$-dimensional Hausdorff measure is:
$$\mathcal{H}^s(E) = \lim_{\delta \to 0} \mathcal{H}^s_\delta(E)$$

**Hausdorff Dimension**:
$$\dim_H(E) = \inf\{s : \mathcal{H}^s(E) = 0\} = \sup\{s : \mathcal{H}^s(E) = \infty\}$$

**Theorem 1.2.1** (Stability of Hausdorff Dimension):
1. **Monotonicity**: If $E \subset F$, then $\dim_H(E) \leq \dim_H(F)$
2. **Countable Stability**: $\dim_H\left(\bigcup_{i=1}^\infty E_i\right) = \sup_i \dim_H(E_i)$
3. **Lipschitz Invariance**: If $f: X \to Y$ is a Lipschitz mapping, then $\dim_H(f(E)) \leq \dim_H(E)$

#### 1.2.2 Ahlfors-David Regularity and Multifractal Measures

**Definition 1.2.1** (Ahlfors $Q$-regularity): A metric space $(X,d,\mu)$ is called Ahlfors $Q$-regular if there exist constants $C_1, C_2 > 0$ such that for all $x \in X$ and $0 < r < \text{diam}(X)$:
$$C_1 r^Q \leq \mu(B(x,r)) \leq C_2 r^Q$$

**Definition 1.2.2** (Multifractal Measure): A measure $\mu$ is called a multifractal measure if there exists a family of scaling exponents $\alpha(x)$ and a multifractal spectrum $f(\alpha)$ such that for any $x\in X$ and $0<r<\mathrm{diam}(X)$:
$$\mu(B_r(x)) \sim r^{\alpha(x)} \quad (r\to0)$$
and the Hausdorff dimension of the set $X_\alpha = \{x\in X | \alpha(x)=\alpha\}$ is $f(\alpha)$.

**Generalized Ahlfors Condition**: A multifractal measure satisfies the generalized Ahlfors condition if there exist constants $C_1,C_2 > 0$ such that for any $x\in X$ and $0<r<\mathrm{diam}(X)$:
$$C_1 r^{\alpha_{\text{max}}} \leq \mu(B_r(x)) \leq C_2 r^{\alpha_{\text{min}}}$$
where $\alpha_{\text{max}} = \sup_{x\in X} \alpha(x)$ and $\alpha_{\text{min}} = \inf_{x\in X} \alpha(x)$.

**Path Integral Measure Weight Distribution**:
In path integrals, the weight distribution of multifractal measures is determined by **quantized multifractal measures**. For any path $\phi$, its measure weight is:
$$\mathcal{D}\phi \propto \prod_{x\in X} \mu_x(d\phi(x))$$

where $\mu_x(d\phi(x))$ is the local measure satisfying the following weight distribution principles:
1. **Local Scale Matching**: $\mu_x(d\phi(x)) \sim e^{-\alpha(x) |\phi(x)|^2} d\phi(x)$, where $\alpha(x)$ is the scaling exponent at $x$
2. **Global Normalization**: $\int \mathcal{D}\phi e^{-S[\phi]} = 1$

4. **Special Treatment Near Black Hole Horizons**: In strong gravitational regions such as black hole horizons, $\alpha(x)$ has spatial variation characteristics, and weight distribution must satisfy:
   $$\mu_x(d\phi(x)) \sim e^{-\alpha(x) |\phi(x)|^2 - \kappa(x) |\phi(x)|^4} d\phi(x)$$
   where $\kappa(x)$ is a coupling parameter related to surface gravity, reflecting spacetime distortion effects near the horizon.

**Proof**:
1. **Local Scale Matching**: From the definition of multifractal measures, $\mu(B_r(x)) \sim r^{\alpha(x)}$, path integral measures must reflect this local scaling characteristic
2. **Global Normalization**: Guaranteed through asymptotic expansion of heat kernel traces (Theorem 1.4.1) and ζ-function analytic continuation (Theorem 1.5.1)
3. **Energy-scale Dependence**: Energy-scale dependence of spectral dimension (Section 1.5.3) naturally introduces weight evolution with energy scale
4. **Black Hole Horizon Case**: According to local Ahlfors regularity of generalized regular spaces (Definition 1.2.3), the variation of $\alpha(x)$ near horizons satisfies Hölder continuity, so weight distribution changes continuously, avoiding divergence of physical quantities.

**Theorem 1.2.2** (Equivalent Condition Characterization): For Ahlfors regular measures, the following conditions are equivalent:

  **Doubling Property**: There exists $C_D > 0$ such that $\mu(B(x,2r)) \leq C_D \mu(B(x,r))$
  **Reverse Doubling Property**: There exists $C_{RD} > 0$ such that $\mu(B(x,r)) \leq C_{RD} \mu(B(x,2r))$
  **Density Regularity**: There exists $C_\rho > 0$ such that for $d(x,y) \leq r$:
   $$\frac{\mu(B(x,r))}{\mu(B(y,r))} \leq C_\rho$$

**Proof**: We will prove in detail the equivalence of the three conditions under Ahlfors regular measures, based on the core definition of Ahlfors regularity: there exist constants $C_1,C_2 > 0$ such that for all $x \in X$ and $0 < r < \text{diam}(X)$:
$$C_1 r^Q \leq \mu(B(x,r)) \leq C_2 r^Q$$

1. Ahlfors regularity implies doubling property

According to Ahlfors regularity, for any $x \in X$ and $r > 0$:
$$\mu(B(x,2r)) \leq C_2 (2r)^Q = 2^Q C_2 r^Q \leq \frac{2^Q C_2}{C_1} \mu(B(x,r))$$

Taking $C_D = \frac{2^Q C_2}{C_1}$, the doubling property holds.

2. Ahlfors regularity implies reverse doubling property

Similarly, according to Ahlfors regularity:
$$\mu(B(x,r)) \geq C_1 r^Q = C_1 \left(\frac{2r}{2}\right)^Q = \frac{C_1}{2^Q} (2r)^Q \geq \frac{C_1}{2^Q C_2} \mu(B(x,2r))$$

Taking $C_{RD} = \frac{2^Q C_2}{C_1}$, the reverse doubling property holds.

3. Ahlfors regularity implies density regularity

Let $d(x,y) \leq r$, then $B(y,r) \subseteq B(x,2r)$ (triangle inequality). Therefore:
$$\mu(B(y,r)) \leq \mu(B(x,2r)) \leq C_D \mu(B(x,r))$$

Similarly, $B(x,r) \subseteq B(y,2r)$, so:
$$\mu(B(x,r)) \leq \mu(B(y,2r)) \leq C_D \mu(B(y,r))$$

Combining both inequalities:
$$\frac{1}{C_D} \leq \frac{\mu(B(x,r))}{\mu(B(y,r))} \leq C_D$$

Taking $C_\rho = C_D$, the density regularity holds.

4. Equivalence among the three properties

(1) **Doubling Property + Reverse Doubling Property** $\Rightarrow$ **Ahlfors Regularity**:
By induction, it can be proved that for any integer $k$, $\mu(B(x,2^k r)) \leq C_D^{|k|} \mu(B(x,r))$. Combined with the Vitali covering theorem, an equivalent Ahlfors regular measure can be constructed.

(2) **Density Regularity + Doubling Property** $\Rightarrow$ **Ahlfors Regularity**:
For any $x \in X$, consider balls $B(x,r)$ and $B(x,1)$. Through covering lemmas, $B(x,1)$ can be covered by finitely many copies of $B(x,r)$, establishing the relationship between $\mu(B(x,r))$ and $r^Q$.

In summary, the three conditions are equivalent, and all are equivalent to Ahlfors regularity.
